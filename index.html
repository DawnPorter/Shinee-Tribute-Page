Here's a time line of Shinees Career:

  • 1989 - Lee Jinki (Onew) is Born December 14th
  • 1990 - Kim Jonghyun is born April 9th
  • 1991 - Kim Kibum (Key) is born September 23rd and Choi Minho is born December 9th
  • 1993 - Lee Taemin is born July 18th
  • 2005 - Jonghyun was discovered at 2005 S.M. Casting System. Kibum auditioned at 2005 S.M. National Tour Audition Casting. Taemin auditioned at "2005 S.M. Open Weekend Audition Casting.
  • 2006 - Jinki and Minho auditioned at 2006 S.M. Academy Casting
  • 2008 - Shinee Debuts on May 25, 2008, the group's first mini-album, "Replay" was released from Label company, SM Entertainment. The mini-album debuted at #10 on the Korean music charts and peaked at #8, selling 17,957 copies in the first half of 2008.
  • 2009 - On February of 2009, SM Entertainment announced that Shinee would make their comeback with their second mini-album, Romeo, on May 21, 2009. It was delayed until May 25th due to Onew damaging his teeth. Five months after the release of "Romeo", Shinee released their third mini-album. SM Entertainment stated that this EP would showcase the group's vocal talents and demonstrate their unique qualities. The lead single "Ring Ding Dong" was released 5 days earlier.
  • 2010 - Five months after the release of "Romeo", Shinee released their third mini-album. SM Entertainment stated that this EP would showcase the group's vocal talents and demonstrate their unique qualities. The lead single "Ring Ding Dong" was released a few days earlier. The studio album re-released a repackaged version entitled, "Hello".Four days after the album was released, the video for the Promotional video also titled "Hello" was released.
  • 2011 Shinee had their Japan Debut Premium Reception in London at the Abbey Road Studios. Shinee started their Japan Debut Premium Reception Tour. They performed at a number of concerts while touring various cities across Japan including Fukuoka on July 22, Kobe on July 23, Tokyo on July 27 and 28, Sapporo on August 8 and Nagoya on August 11, 2011
  • 2012 - Shinee's fourth EP, Sherlock, was released in 2012. The lead single "Sherlock (Clue + Note)" claimed the top spot on the Gaon Digital Chart and charted third on the Korea K-Pop Hot 100 chart and became the group's highest-selling digital single in South Korea, with over 1.7 million digital downloads.
  • 2013 - Shinee released two-part studio albums Dream Girl – The Misconceptions of You and Why So Serious? – The Misconceptions of Me, which were later re-released as The Misconceptions of Us (2013). The same year, the group released their fifth EP Everybody and another number one single "Everybody". Shinee was one of the biggest-selling K-pop acts in America in 2013, becoming one of the first Korean musicians to rank on Billboard's year-end World Albums chart.
  • 2014 - Shinee announces their first ever solo group concert at Tokyo Dome.
  • 2015 - March 14 and 15, 2015, Shinee performed at the Tokyo Dome for the finale performance of their Shinee World 2014 tour (promoted as SHINEE WORLD 2014 ~I'M YOUR BOY~ IN TOKYO DOME). The tour was attended by a total of 300,000 fans. Shinee released their fourth studio album Odd with the lead single "View" which claimed the top of the charts. The reissue of the album, Married to the Music, was released the same year with four additional songs.
  • 2016 - SM Entertainment confirmed on July 22, 2016, that Shinee would be kicking off their fifth concert tour, Shinee World V, in September 2016. Originally, there were to be two concerts held on September 3 and 4 at the Olympic Gymnastics Arena in Seoul, but a third was added for September 2 after heavy demand for ticketing. throguh 2016-2017 Shinee help concerts in Yokohama, Dallas, Los Angeles, Toronto, Vancouver, Hong-Kong and Bangkok
  • 2017 - On June 22, 2017, concerts for SHINee WORLD 2017 ~FIVE~ Special Edition in Tokyo Dome and Osaka Dome were announced respectively. With the addition of the dome concerts, the tour wrapped up with a total of 29 shows and a total attendance of 539,000. This was their last tour with member Jonghyun who died on December 18, 2017.
  • 2018 - After Jonghyun's death on December 18, 2017, the remaining members entered into discussions on whether to postpone the concerts and decided to hold them as scheduled. Shinee World The Best 2018 (promoted as "SHINee WORLD THE BEST 2018～FROM NOW ON～") was the sixth Japan concert tour by South Korean boy band Shinee. This tour was described as a culmination of Shinee's activities in Japan since debut, kicking off in Kyocera Dome Osaka on February 17, 2018, and ending in Tokyo Dome on February 27, 2018. In the concerts, Jonghyun appeared posthumously using isolated vocal tracks and clips from previous performances being played on the video screens. For the final encore, "From Now On", which was the final track he recorded with the group, the remaining members performed with an empty space and microphone stand in the middle of the four, which was illuminated while Jonghyun's vocals were played on tape.
  • 2018 - In May 2018, SM Entertainment announced Shinee would return with a new album to celebrate the group's tenth anniversary, The Story of Light is the sixth studio album by South Korean boy group Shinee. This album release marks the group's first in South Korea since 1 of 1 in November 2016, and the first since member Jonghyun's death in December 2017. It is the last Shinee release to feature Jonghyun following his death. SM later confirms Onews military enlistment for December.
  • 2019 - Key and Minho enlist in the Military
  • 2021 - Taemin enlists in the Military
